Morningtide Services LLC – Project Role

This two-pager outlines how Morningtide Services LLC will function as a financing vehicle and services company for modular BSFL units installed at Turtle Creek Farm, and how it participates economically alongside White Eagle Nutrition LLC and its brands: Nellie’s BSFL, Nellie’s Garden, White Eagle (pet), and Luxitin.

Financing Vehicle Services Company Waste-to-Value Multi-Brand Offtake
Page 1 · Structure & Roles

1. Purpose

Morningtide Services LLC is intended to:

  • Provide equipment financing for modular BSFL units and ancillary infrastructure deployed at Turtle Creek Farm.
  • Serve as a services company that provides processing labor and operational support for those units.
  • Earn interest income on equipment financing, labor service fees, and a long-term equity stake in White Eagle Nutrition LLC.

White Eagle Nutrition is the platform entity owning:

  • Nellie’s BSFL – modular BSFL systems and ingredient supply.
  • Nellie’s Garden – frass-based soil and garden products.
  • White Eagle Nutrition (pet) – BSFL-based pet and livestock nutrition.
  • Luxitin – chitin-based haircare and biodegradable packaging commercialization brand.
Reference sites: nelliesbsfl.com nelliesgarden.com whiteeaglenutrition.com luxitin.com

2. Parties & High-Level Roles

Turtle Creek Farm
Fish Farm · Site Host
Organic waste provider Feed & frass off-taker Site & utilities

Role: Provides a defined portion of its organic waste streams as feedstock to the BSFL units, receives a negotiated share of BSFL and frass, and may pay tipping/processing fees or grant favorable site rights in exchange for discounted feed and frass.

  • Allocates waste streams (e.g., mortalities, offal, fines) that meet agreed specifications.
  • Provides space, utilities, and ongoing operational coordination with farm operations.
  • Receives discounted BSFL feed and frass for internal on-farm use.
White Eagle Nutrition LLC
HoldCo · Project Sponsor
Nellie’s BSFL Nellie’s Garden White Eagle (pet) Luxitin

Role: Serves as the project sponsor and offtake platform. Owns the brand family, executes offtake agreements for BSFL, frass, and chitin streams, and grants Morningtide a small equity stake.

  • Nellie’s BSFL: designs and oversees modular BSFL units at Turtle Creek.
  • Nellie’s Garden: markets frass as retail and wholesale soil products.
  • White Eagle (pet): buys BSFL ingredients for pet and livestock formulations.
  • Luxitin: sources chitin-rich fractions from BSFL co-products for haircare and packaging.
Morningtide Services LLC
Financing & Services Vehicle
Equipment financing On-site processing labor Service fees Minority equity

Role: Provides a portion of equipment financing and processing labor for the Turtle Creek modular units. Earns interest income, service fees, and a small equity stake in White Eagle Nutrition LLC.

  • Finances a portion of modular BSFL units and ancillary equipment.
  • Employs or contracts on-site processing staff under a Services Agreement.
  • Holds a minority equity interest in White Eagle aligned with long-term growth.

3. Project Structure at Turtle Creek Farm

3.1 Capex & Ownership

Morningtide finances a defined portion of:

  • Modular BSFL units (hardware, racks, bins, sensors, IoT).
  • Site preparation and utilities tie-ins required for BSFL operations.
  • Ancillary handling equipment (carts, totes, simple processing line, etc.).

Legal implementation may take one of two primary structures:

  • Debt structure: Morningtide lends to White Eagle (or a project SPV) and retains a security interest in the equipment.
  • Lease structure: Morningtide owns the equipment and leases it back under an Equipment Lease.

White Eagle (directly or via Nellie’s BSFL) is responsible for unit design, commissioning, and performance.

3.2 Operating Responsibilities

  • Turtle Creek Farm provides:
    • Waste streams meeting agreed specifications and biosecurity standards.
    • Space, utilities, and ongoing operational coordination with farm activity.
  • Morningtide Services LLC:
    • Employs or contracts on-site processing staff.
    • Handles daily tasks: waste receipt, larvae feeding, harvesting, basic processing, and cleanup.
    • Manages shift scheduling and on-site productivity KPIs under a Services Agreement.
  • Nellie’s BSFL / White Eagle:
    • Sets biological performance targets and QA/QC standards.
    • Provides SOPs, training, remote monitoring, and periodic audits.

3.3 Offtake & Product Flows

The BSFL modular units convert Turtle Creek’s waste into:

  • BSFL Whole/Dried/Meal
    • Portion allocated to Turtle Creek as discounted on-farm feed.
    • Balance sold via White Eagle to its own brands and external ingredient buyers.
  • Frass
    • Portion allocated to Turtle Creek for soil, land, and/or pond health.
    • Balance transferred to Nellie’s Garden for packaging and resale.
  • Chitin / Co-products
    • Defined fraction of high-purity chitin-rich material transferred to Luxitin as ingredient feedstock.

4. Cash Flows & Economics

4.1 From Turtle Creek Farm

  • In-kind contribution: organic waste streams, site access, and cooperation with operational SOPs.
  • Payments / consideration:
    • Either a tipping/processing fee paid to the project, or
    • Discounted feed and frass pricing in lieu of cash tipping fees.
  • Value received:
    • Discounted BSFL-based feed for fish and/or poultry.
    • Frass for soil, land, and pond health.
    • Reduced waste-handling and disposal costs and improved ESG positioning.

4.2 From White Eagle / Brand Offtakers

  • Offtake payments:
    • White Eagle (pet) buys BSFL ingredients at transfer prices that support debt service, operating costs, and project margin.
    • Nellie’s Garden buys or receives frass at internal prices and captures downstream retail/wholesale margin.
    • Luxitin purchases chitin-rich fractions at prices reflecting higher processing costs and premium status.
  • Equity grant to Morningtide: White Eagle grants Morningtide a small equity stake (common or preferred) in recognition of project financing and ongoing services that de-risk execution and scale.

4.3 Returns to Morningtide Services LLC

Component 1 – Interest Income (Financing Arm)

  • Principal: cost of financed equipment and initial project capital.
  • Terms: agreed interest rate, amortization, and security (e.g., lien on equipment).
  • Repayment source: free cash flow from BSFL, frass, and chitin sales under offtake agreements.

Component 2 – Labor & Services Revenue (Operating Arm)

  • Morningtide signs a Services Agreement with White Eagle (or a project SPV).
  • Covers on-site labor for processing, cleaning, and routine maintenance.
  • Compensation structure:
    • Fixed monthly service fee covering base labor and supervision.
    • Optional volume-based performance bonuses tied to tons processed or larvae yields.

Component 3 – Equity Upside (Stake in White Eagle Nutrition)

  • Morningtide holds a minority equity interest in White Eagle Nutrition LLC.
  • Value created through:
    • Expansion of BSFL ingredient volumes across multiple sites.
    • Scaling of Nellie’s Garden and Luxitin branded products.
    • Growth of White Eagle’s direct-to-consumer and wholesale channels.
  • Potential exit via dividends, secondary sale, recapitalization, or sale to a strategic buyer.
